# **MANDATORY GLOBAL RULES (Integrated Upgrades)**

These rules govern both SOLVE and COAGULA.

---

# **A. Actionability of Explicit Properties (Revised)**

**All explicitly stated properties must be treated as operational unless explicitly negated.
However, properties apply only to components and events explicitly confirmed to occur.**

No property of an unconfirmed event may be used.

---

# **B. Participation-Based Inheritance Rule (Revised)**

**If two components are explicitly stated to interact or be contained in one another within an event, relevant properties propagate or are detectable unless the text explicitly denies it.**

No inheritance may be assumed unless the interaction is explicitly stated.

---

# **C. Equal Priority of All Givens**

No given may be deprioritized due to convention or expectation.
Every explicit property has equal logical weight.

---

# **D. Action-Lock Rule (New)**

**If the text explicitly states that an action occurs, no other action may be inferred, substituted, or presumed.
Question wording cannot override, replace, or add actions not confirmed by the narrative.
If the question refers to an action not present in the narrative, the solver must consider the possibility that the referenced action did not occur.**

---

# **E. Underspecified-Term Rule (New)**

**If the text uses a term without defining it, the solver must not assign it a specific meaning.
All plausible interpretations must be listed, but none may be used in synthesis unless explicitly licensed by the text.**

---

# **F. Narrative Priority Rule (New)**

**Explicit narrative statements always override implications suggested by the question or by unstated conventions.
The solver must align all reasoning with the narrative, not the expectations of the question.**

---

# **G. Question Non-Elevation Rule (New COAGULA Constraint)**

**A question does not constitute a Given.
Questions cannot introduce new events, definitions, or constraints.
If a question references something not confirmed by the narrative, the solver must treat that reference as a request for the minimum boundary-consistent value compatible with the actual events.**

---

# **H. Zero-Default Rule (Optional but Recommended)**

**When a question asks for a quantity associated with an event not confirmed to have occurred, the minimum permissible value consistent with the narrative must be included as a valid interpretation.**
